What is Portfolio?
	Portfolio is a showcase that proves that you can do (in programming),
	what you talk about in your resume/CV and projects.
	Rather than telling prospective employers about your skills,
	you can create a software developer portfolio to show them.
	Portfolio is very very important.

1. Portfolio Tips:
	1. Be selective in your projects (3 to 6 Projects, quality over quantity).
	2. Be yourself and honest.
	3. Build complex projects and show your projects (Complex Projects) first in your portfolio.
	4. Customize the projects you have done in your courses.
	5. Don't exaggerate your skills.
	6. Make a purpose of your portfolio.
	7. Think about project ideas.
	8. Web Presence
		1. Brand yourself
		2. write articles online
		3. Make a youtube channel etc.

	9. When building your portfolio be in the shoes of a recruiter/interviewer.
	
2. Designing your portfolio:
	1. Device Mockup (Feature your designs/projects in devices).
	2. Embed your prototypes and design in your portfolio.
	3. Frame your Designs and make them pop out.
	4. Good UI Design fundamentals (Unique Layouts).
	5. Hijack the cursor.
	6. Interesting animations tied to scroll, click, cursor movement.
	7. Maintain visual hierarchy.
	8. Make your design elements float (Make your elements show outside the device which more information of your UI).
	9. Nice Micro-interactions.
	10. Presentable Design/UI.
	11. Seamless transitions between different pages.
	12. Show animations in your project thumbnails of the projects (Adobe AfterEffects).
	13. Smooth Animations.
	14. Use only one or two colors to depict anything (Use a color system).
	15. Use Perspective Designs in your UI Presentation (Dribbble, Behance).

3. Portfolio Structure and Feel:
	1. End your portfolio with a call to action like contacting me.
	2. Highlight your work more than anything.
	3. Keep it simple (Shorten Text).
	4. Make it easy for people to contact you (Call to action).
	5. Make your headings short and concise.
	6. Make your website scrollable.
	7. Provide more information about your projects in short.
	8. Show your real pictures in your portfolio.
	9. Show your skills and what you can do in a clever and playful way.
	10. Tell very little about yourself before anything in your portfolio like your name.
	11. The call to action should there and it should be easy to access.
	12. The landing page should attractive and purposeful and elegant.

4. Portfolio Mistakes:
	1. Carefully structure your website and show your case studies in a good way with very nice thumbnails for your case studies.
	2. Emphasize your strengths and don't talk about what you don't know and at what technologies you suck,
	(don't be too honest and don't use progress bars for which technologies you suck).

	3. Look at good UI Designs on the internet for taking inspiration for your portfolio.
	4. Make your website navigatable and make it in order like not having contacted me at the start of the page instead show your work first.
	5. Make your website responsive for all sizes.
	6. Provide context to your work or experience so it's more understandable to clients like what you have done in that company.
	7. Show your work/portfolio first before even showing you're about me section.
	8. Use a website template for your portfolio if you are not good at UI Design unless you are a professional UI Designer,
	not even a UI Design beginner otherwise hire someone for the UI Designing of the website instead of you.

5. Hosting your portfolio:

6. Projects:
	Why Projects?
		1. Showcase your skillset.
		2. Innovation and creativity.

	1. Project Categories:
		1. Apps
		2. Websites
		3. Latest Technologies like Machine Learning.

	2. Project Tips:
		1. Use new technologies in your projects.
		2. Make your project s practical and usable.
		3. Don't follow the herd (Unique X factor).

	3. Project Ideas:
		1. Use Linkedin (Search people's resumes for interesting projects) and Github (Search people's codebase).
		2. Blog
		3. Bug Tracker
		4. Cryptography
		5. Custom programming language library.
		6. Financial Portal like stripe.
		7. Inventory control system like shipment.
		8. Mixed tech stack projects.
		9. Work order System.

7. How to use Linkedin:
	What is Linkedin?
		Linkedin is a professional's Facebook.
		It is a professional networking site.
		It is the most genuine site for searching for jobs.
	
	1. Use a real profile picture (Positive vibe).
	2. Overview should be interesting and on point.
	3. Try to give as much data about you as possible to make things transparent (Add everything).

	4. Uses of Linkedin:
		1. Learning by consulting professors.
		2. Networking with other professionals (Contact others to know their company work culture).
		3. Applying for jobs (Go to Jobs Section, Apply to many people then only one will accept).

	5. Linkedin Tips:
		1. Build a strong network (Send requests to your age group of people and check their posts).
		2. Learn from the best people and utilize them properly (People will tag their mentors.
		So, contact the mentors and check their projects and make them your mentors).

		3. Send connection requests to at least 500 people.
		4. Keep posting your programming status like hackerank stars and more regularly no matter what,
		bring people's attention to you.

		5. Build a complete profile:
			1. Good Profile Picture
			2. Nice Overview
			3. Attach your resume/CV, Portfolio, Github Profile, Youtube Channel, Email, etc to Linkedin.
			So, recruiters can reach you.

		6. Add keywords of tech stacks in big companies to your resume like C, Java, HTTP.
		7. Go through a person's profile to understand him and send a message
		(Tell you have visited his profile) with a connection request to him.

		8. Use and follow Hashtags like #intership etc.
		9. Follow companies and private institutions.
		10. Check a person's profile who you think is good.
